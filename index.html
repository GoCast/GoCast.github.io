GoCast It!

Group collaboration using WebRTC technologies and XMPP

Welcome to GoCast

We are a small startup dedicated to small group collaboration and advancing the WebRTC cause. Our company splits down the line of a commercial product around collaboration and the WebRTC core for audio and video components of that collaboration inside the confines of a web browser.

Small Group Collaboration

Our small group collaboration software can be found on our main site at GoCast.it where you can jump into a sample room and try it out. More information about the product can also be found at the company website. Our product utilizes standard XMPP servers in conjunction with a good amount of Node.JS with custom XMPP clients acting in a number of roles which dictate client interaction, access controls and make bridges to the backend NoSQL databases on Amazon AWS.

WebRTC efforts

WebRTC is a great audio/video technology which is being integrated into browsers over time. Google started the WebRTC project and has native support for it in Chrome version 24+. Firefox is building WebRTC natively in the May/June 2013 timeframe. And Opera is working on native support as well. Other browsers may or may not support WebRTC directly. At GoCast, we had a need - a need for WebRTC support in multiple browsers on multiple platforms in a much earlier timeframe... so we built a plugin which wraps the WebRTC framework and allows us to have access to all the great WebRTC features on Windows, Mac, and Linux(*YMMV) and currently on Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and (not fully tested) IE among others who support the NSAPI plugin mechanism.

We had an open source effort in 2012 which is now being updated here in a much more organized fashion. We have a much simpler build mechanism and we have Javascript support for seamless use of the native or plugin version of WebRTC which further simplifies your life as a would-be application developer wanting peer-to-peer video and audio real-time communications. Woo hoo!
